About my projects

Biosensors: Heavy Metal Sensing

  • Our project focuses on developing an accurate biosensor capable of detecting multiple heavy metals.
  • The sensor design incorporates a novel, different droplet formation that shows the fluorescent property indicating the presence of heavy metal.
  • As part of my project on heavy metal sensing, I developed and optimized various types of microdroplets that exhibit fluorescent properties.
  • These specialized droplets serve as sensitive platforms for detecting trace amounts of heavy metals.

Thermo-cells: Means to generate Electricity

Jan 2022 - May 2022

  • Our project studies thermo-cells as a means to generate electricity - these are the chemistry versions of solid-state thermo-electric cells.
  • Heat is an inherent part of gasoline-powered (e.g., engines), or sustainable generators (e.g., solar, or wind).
  • Heat is also generated from wastelands and the project aims to use the, otherwise wasted thermal energy, as an additional electrical resource.
  • I developed sophisticated COMSOL Multiphysics models. My simulation framework incorporates coupled heat transfer, mass transport, electrochemical reactions, and fluid dynamics to accurately predict cell performance under various operating conditions.
